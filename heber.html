Heber City

Heber City offers a blend of scenic beauty, historical significance, and community warmth, making it a beloved destination and home in the heart of Utah's Mountain West.

Facts

      Heber Created

      Incorporated in 1889, Heber City has a rich history tied to its agricultural roots and later growth spurred by mining and tourism. Today, it serves as a gateway to the Uinta Mountains and is known for its stunning scenery, including the nearby Deer Creek Reservoir and Provo River.

      Heber's Population

      Heber City, settled in the beautiful Heber Valley of Utah, exemplifies the charm and natural beauty of the Wasatch Back region of the Mountain West. With a population of approximately 17,000 residents, Heber City offers a serene suburban, community atmosphere.

      Heber's Average Income

      Located about 45 minutes southeast of Salt Lake City, Heber City enjoys a tranquil setting amidst rugged mountains, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Heber City boasts a strong community spirit with local events like the Swiss Days festival, celebrating its pioneer heritage with traditional food, music, and crafts. The city's average income sits around $46,000 for a single person, reflecting its mix of small-town charm and economic stability, supported by industries such as tourism, agriculture, and retail.
