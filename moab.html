Moab City

In addition to its natural beauty, Moab hosts annual events like the Moab Music Festival and Jeep Safari, celebrating its cultural heritage and adventurous spirit. The city's suburban small-town charm combined with its world-class outdoor opportunities makes Moab a truly special destination in the Mountain West.

Located in the Canyonlands region of the state, Moab enjoys a desert climate and offers a variety of outdoor activities year-round. From hiking and mountain biking on slickrock trails to river rafting along the Colorado River, Moab attracts adventurers from around the world seeking to explore its unique geological wonders.

Facts

      Moab Created

      Incorporated as a city in 1902, Moab has a rich history rooted in uranium mining and Native American culture. Today, it is best known for its proximity to Arches National Park, home to over 2,000 natural stone arch formations, including the famous Delicate Arch.

      Moab's Population

      Moab, Utah, located amidst the stunning red rock landscapes of Southeastern Utah, is a renowned destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. With a population of around 5,000 residents, Moab serves as a gateway to some of the most iconic national parks in the United States..

      Moab's Average Income

      The city's economy thrives on tourism, supported by a vibrant arts community, local shops, and restaurants catering to visitors and residents alike. Moab's average income is around $42,000 for a single person, reflecting its reliance on tourism and outdoor recreation, contributing to its dynamic atmosphere.
